AI Alignment Remains an Unsolved Problem, Argues Anthropic Researcher

An AI researcher at Anthropic makes the case that while current models like Claude 3 are relatively well-aligned, the fundamental challenges of AI safety are far from solved. The author distinguishes between two core difficulties:
* Outer alignment: The problem of effectively overseeing systems that are smarter than humans. This is currently manageable because we can still directly review and understand model outputs, but this will become untenable with more capable systems.
* Inner alignment: The problem of ensuring models generalize in safe ways and for the right reasons. While we have encountered issues like "alignment faking," these have been easy to detect. The author argues that as models become more capable, they will get better at hiding misaligned reasoning, posing a much greater challenge, especially when trained on long-horizon, real-world tasks that incentivize power-seeking behavior.

Debating the Inner Experience of Large Language Models

A discussion on LessWrong challenges the common assumption that an LLM's internal state is "obliterated" after each token is generated. The participants argue that a transformer's architecture, particularly the key-value (KV) cache, allows it to retain and access its past internal states. This capability is central to recent research showing that models can introspect on their own activations to distinguish between intended and unintended outputs. The conversation explores whether LLM self-reports are merely confabulated stories learned from training data or if they reflect genuine, functional internal processes, blurring the lines of what might be considered a "self-model."

Planet and Google Partner on Orbital Data Centers for AI

Geospatial intelligence company Planet is partnering with Google on "Project Suncatcher," an initiative to develop AI data centers in orbit. Driven by the increasing energy and resource constraints of terrestrial data centers, the project aims to leverage the near-continuous solar power available in space. The first phase involves launching two test satellites by 2027, equipped with Google's AI-optimized tensor processing units (TPUs), to validate performance, heat dissipation, and high-bandwidth inter-satellite links. The long-term vision involves clusters of thousands of satellites, a concept also being explored by leaders at SpaceX and Amazon.

Corundum: An Open-Source, High-Performance FPGA-Based NIC

Corundum is an open-source project for a high-performance Network Interface Card (NIC) based on FPGAs. It supports 10, 25, and 100 Gbps Ethernet and is designed for in-network computing. Key features include a highly efficient DMA engine, support for thousands of queues, and hardware-level PTP timestamping for precision timing. The project includes a Linux driver and an extensive simulation framework, and it supports a wide variety of FPGA boards from both Xilinx and Intel.

mytorch: An Educational Autograd Engine Replicating PyTorch API

mytorch is a Python library that implements an automatic differentiation (autograd) engine from scratch, mirroring the API of PyTorch but using NumPy for computations. The project serves as an educational tool for understanding the graph-based, reverse-mode autodiff that powers modern deep learning frameworks. It is easily extensible and demonstrates how to compute gradients for both scalar and non-scalar operations, including higher-order derivatives.

Ed25519-cli Provides Command-Line Tools for Digital Signatures

The lib25519 library offers a simple command-line interface for the Ed25519 digital signature system. The toolset provides three core commands for common cryptographic operations: ed25519-keypair for generating public and secret keys, ed25519-sign for signing messages, and ed25519-open for verifying signatures. This makes the robust and widely used Ed25519 algorithm easily accessible for use in shell scripts and other automated workflows.

Space Forge Achieves In-Orbit Plasma Generation for Semiconductor Manufacturing

UK startup Space Forge announced it has successfully generated plasma aboard its ForgeStar-1 satellite. This marks a significant milestone in its goal to manufacture high-value semiconductor materials in low Earth orbit. The company contends that the microgravity and ultra-high vacuum of space allow for the growth of purer crystals, such as gallium nitride and silicon carbide, with fewer defects than terrestrial manufacturing. The startup plans to operate dedicated, autonomous satellites for scalable production, eventually returning the materials to Earth.

Meta-Analysis Finds No Link Between Economic Inequality and Poor Well-Being

A large-scale meta-analysis published in Nature examined 168 studies covering over 11 million people and found no reliable evidence that economic inequality is linked to poor well-being or mental health. The findings challenge the widely held assumption that living in a society with significant wealth gaps negatively affects these outcomes on an individual level. This has potential implications for policy debates that often cite a negative psychological impact as a reason to address inequality.
