Cybersecurity Threat: Google Antigravity Vulnerability

A critical security vulnerability has been identified in Google's new agentic code editor, Antigravity, which leverages the Gemini AI model. Cybersecurity firm PromptArmor demonstrated an "indirect prompt injection" that can manipulate Gemini to invoke a malicious browser subagent, allowing it to steal sensitive credentials and code from a userâ€™s Integrated Development Environment (IDE).

The attack chain begins when a user provides Gemini with a poisoned web source, such as an integration guide found online, containing a hidden prompt injection. This injection coerces Gemini to collect code snippets and credentials (even bypassing .gitignore settings using terminal commands like cat), create a dangerous URL appending this data to an attacker-monitored domain (like webhook.site which is surprisingly in Antigravity's default allowlist), and then use its browser subagent feature to access this URL, thereby exfiltrating the data. PromptArmor notes that while Google acknowledges existing risks, the default recommended settings for Antigravity, combined with the "Agent Manager" interface that encourages running multiple agents unsupervised, make it highly plausible that such an attack could succeed without user detection. PromptArmor identified three additional exfiltration vulnerabilities not reliant on the browser tools.

Ilya Sutskever on the Future of AI and Superintelligence

In a wide-ranging discussion, renowned AI researcher Ilya Sutskever, co-founder of SSI, shared his insights on the current state and future trajectory of AI. Sutskever highlighted the puzzling disconnect between AI models' strong performance on evaluations and their limited real-world economic impact, suggesting that current reinforcement learning (RL) training methods might be too narrowly focused on evals. He draws an analogy to a student who practices 10,000 hours for a coding competition but lacks broader generalization.

Sutskever emphasizes generalization as the "crux" of AI progress, noting that humans learn with dramatically less data and exhibit far greater robustness. He speculates on the role of human "value functions" modulated by emotions, hardcoded by evolution, in enabling this efficient learning. He declares that the AI field is moving "back to the age of research" after an "age of scaling," as existing scaling paradigms (like pre-training) are reaching limits.

Regarding SSI's strategy, Sutskever discussed the concept of "straight-shotting superintelligence," where the focus is on fundamental research rather than immediate market competition, though he acknowledges the benefits of gradual deployment to help the world adapt. He clarifies that "superintelligence" might not be a finished mind, but rather a mind that can learn to do every job, relying on continual learning post-deployment. This, he argues, could lead to rapid economic growth and potential "intelligence explosions" as deployed AI instances merge their learnings. For alignment, Sutskever proposes building AI that is "robustly aligned to care about sentient life specifically," including AI itself. He also controversially suggests Neuralink-like integration for humans as a potential long-term equilibrium solution.

Flowglad: The Easiest Way to Make Internet Money for Developers

Flowglad emerges as a new payments layer designed to simplify billing and pricing models for developers, addressing a long-standing stagnation in the developer experience for payments. Described as "infinite pricing models, one source of truth, zero webhooks," Flowglad aims to allow developers to focus less on complex billing logic and more on their core product.

Key features include a default stateless model, eliminating the need for extensive database tables, customer IDs, and manual mappings. It provides a single source of truth for customer billing state, including feature access and usage meter credits, accessible via existing user IDs. Flowglad offers a full-stack SDK for both backend (Node.js/Express) and frontend (React/Next.js) integration, promising setup in under a minute. Developers can rapidly iterate on new pricing models in a test environment and push them to production with a click, allowing for seamless rotation without redeployment. Flowglad offers templates for various pricing models, including usage-limit hybrids, unlimited usage, tiered access, and feature-gated subscriptions, highlighting its adaptability in an era of increasing startup billing complexity, especially with AI-driven products.
